What I Have Done

A petition initiative — based on 2,754 signatures, a mass comment was submitted to the new Higher Education Act entering into force on 1 September 2026. See: Petition

Successfully negotiated a shortened summer semester (13 instead of 14 weeks) and regularly provide updates on the activities of the Student Council for Higher Education.

The recognition of a Master’s thesis or dissertation as equivalent to a rigorous thesis has successfully been incorporated into §61(1). Legal wording:

“A higher education institution may determine that it recognizes a defended Master’s thesis or a defended dissertation as a rigorous thesis and a successfully completed dissertation defense as a rigorous examination; details shall be laid down in the internal regulation of the higher education institution.”

The conditions for recognition require clarification in the university's internal regulations.

What I Offer

01 — Recognition of Master’s or Doctoral Thesis as a Rigorous Thesis

I am a co-initiator of the mass comment supported by nearly 3,000 students and legal professionals, aimed at recognizing Master’s and Doctoral theses as rigorous theses. Although our proposal made it into the law (effective from 1 Sept 2026), it must still be implemented at the university and faculty level — something I have extensive experience with. For example: Rules of Procedure of the Student Council for Higher Education (authored by me)

02 — Student Kitchen

Would you like to bring food from home and simply heat it up instead of spending more on meals? Our faculty lacks basic kitchen facilities, which are standard at many universities and workplaces. I want to push for establishing a simple, functional student kitchen on campus.

03 — Course Registration System

Every semester, the same problem: the system crashes during registration. AIS2 is managed at the university level, so as a senator in the university senate, I will push for improvements to this outdated system.
